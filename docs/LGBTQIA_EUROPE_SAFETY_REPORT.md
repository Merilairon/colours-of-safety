# LGBTQIA+ Safety Report — Europe

**Report type:** Business Analysis  
**Scope:** All European countries  
**Date:** June 2026  
**Purpose:** Identify safe districts/areas and Points of Interest (POIs) for LGBTQIA+ people, ranked by safety level.

---

## Safety Scale

| Score | Label | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 5 | **Safe** | Openly welcoming, legal protections, active queer scene, no reported hostility |
| 4 | **Mostly Safe** | Generally tolerant, legal protections exist, minor incidents possible |
| 3 | **Neutral** | Mixed tolerance, limited legal protections, caution advised in some areas |
| 2 | **Caution** | Limited protections, social hostility common, public displays inadvisable |
| 1 | **Danger** | Hostile environment, criminalisation risk or severe social danger |

> **Data sources:** ILGA-Europe Rainbow Index 2024, Equaldex, RFSL, Rainbow Europe, local LGBTQ+ NGO reports, community travel guides (Spartacus, IGLTA).

## Analyst Notes

1. **Urban vs. Rural divide** is the single strongest predictor of safety — capital cities across even lower-ranked countries typically score 1–2 points higher than the national average.
2. **Tourism areas** (Mykonos, Sitges, Maspalomas, Hvar) often achieve safety score 5 regardless of national context due to economic incentive and tourist demographics.
3. **Russia and Georgia** represent the most severe deteriorations in the last 3 years, with active legal persecution.
4. **Central-Eastern Europe** shows a bifurcation: Czechia, Estonia, Slovenia trending toward Western norms; Poland, Hungary, Slovakia regressing.
5. **War zones (Ukraine)**: Safety data is unreliable; primary concern should be the general conflict risk.
6. This report should be refreshed at minimum **annually** given the pace of legal change across the region.
